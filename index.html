厨房

A small Shanghai kitchen on a working street

Milpas Street feeds people who are on their way somewhere. That shapes how we cook: everything goes in the wok when you order it, portions are built for leftovers, and the price stays where a neighborhood can reach it.

The menu runs two directions at once. Shanghai and Sichuan on one side — water-boiled fish, mapo tofu in the clay pot, twice-cooked pork belly, soup dumplings. American Chinese classics on the other — chow mein, mu shu, egg foo young, done properly.

And then the part people tell their friends about: more than sixty vegetarian dishes, not a single asterisk on a meat menu. Tofu six ways, baked tofu six more, greens, eggplant, snow peas, and every sauce we make available without meat.
